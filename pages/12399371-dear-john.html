Dear John is a book that makes you jealous for sure. Every girl dreams of a perfect boyfriend or just a relationship like the movies. This book is one of those typical relationships except there is a twist. It shows what a real life relationship may look like. In Dear John, the main character John falls in love with a girl names Savannah. However, John, working in the army, has to leave Savannah and back to his job in a couple of weeks. They build up their relationship in that short amount of time, as much as a couple that has been dating for years. While he's in the army, Savannah and John send handwritten letters to each other to remind them how much they still love each other. During this process, many hardships come by such as John's father becoming sick, 9.11, and a bit too long of a separation between Savannah and John. As you go on with the story, you will be able to see how everything turns out; who ends up with who and what. This book is recommended for teenage girls who likes romance books. It will be very different from any other romance book they have read because of the realistic twist in the plot; how every couple does not get the perfect opportunity to love.

John Tyree, the protagonist, falls in love with a girl, Savannah, that he meets on his second leave in the army. After just one day together, they slowly start falling for each-other. Soon enough, they time together comes to an end, as John has to go back to the army. Savannah and him write nearly every day, as they don’t want to miss a moment in each other's live. Later on John goes on his third leave and spends majority of it making up lost time with Savannah. They then, go and visit John’s dad and spend time with him. John and his father once had a very strong relationship which revolved around coins, until one day, when his dad brought them up John had enough about them, and snapped at him. Savannah and John’s father essentially had a stronger relationship than him and John. Then, John returns to his place in the army in the infantry division. As time goes on, John’s next leave approaches sooner and sooner. However, just months before John can return home, disaster happens. 911. All units and divisions must work on this horrific event and fight for their country. Gradually, John and Savannah's letters lessen. Until, one day, Savannah writes to John that their relationship must disband, as she has fallen for someone else. A broken-hearted John, spends the rest of his leave with his father, and tries to bond with him. Without Savannah, John focuses his leisure on his dad, and attempts to spark the relationship they once had.
John Tyree is twenty to twenty-five years old throughout the entire book. He is strong willed, army grunt, who although he doesn't portray it much, is smarter then said. From both working in the army and surfing, he is quite muscular, but not heavy.
This beguiling novel, takes place on the army grounds in Germany and in Iraq. Although when John is home, it take place in Wilmington, North Carolina. Dear John also takes place between 1999 and 2006.
Overall, I would recommend Dear John to anyone who wants to enjoy a gratifying novel about true love, heartbreak, and sacrifice. Nicholas Sparks explains how true love comes from sacrifice and loyalty. Sparks uses both great detail and prior knowledge to capture your emotions through the text.

Dear John, written by Jamie Linden, tells a bittersweet story of love, sacrifice, and the complexities of relationships. While the narrative unfolds at a slower pace, the emotional depth and thought-provoking themes make it a worthwhile read.

The novel follows the journey of John Tyree, a soldier who falls in love with Savannah Curtis during a brief encounter. Their connection blossoms through heartfelt letters as they navigate the challenges of a long-distance relationship. However, as circumstances change and their lives take different paths, their love is put to the test, forcing them to make difficult choices.

Jamie Linden's writing style allows readers to delve into the characters' emotions, bringing authenticity to their experiences. The character development is particularly notable, as readers witness John's growth, his internal conflicts, and his struggle to reconcile his duty with his desires. Savannah, too, is a well-rounded character, with her own dreams and aspirations that are put to the test as their relationship faces challenges.

While the pacing may be slow at times, it allows for a deeper exploration of the characters' inner worlds and the complex dynamics of their relationships. The slower moments provide an opportunity to reflect on the sacrifices made for love and the consequences that follow.

The themes of love, duty, and the impact of war on personal lives are poignant and thought-provoking. Dear John raises important questions about the sacrifices individuals make for their loved ones and the difficult choices they face when confronted with conflicting responsibilities. It invites readers to reflect on the complexities of human relationships and the often unpredictable nature of life.

Although the slower pace may not suit all readers' preferences, the emotional resonance and the exploration of profound themes make Dear John a compelling read. The novel showcases Jamie Linden's ability to capture the intricacies of love and sacrifice, creating an impactful story that lingers in the hearts of readers.

In conclusion, Dear John is a poignant tale that beautifully examines the intricacies of love, duty, and sacrifice. While the slower pacing may require patience, the emotional depth and thought-provoking themes make this novel a worthwhile exploration of the complexities of human relationships.

Note: Dear John was also adapted into a movie, and while the film captures the essence of the story, the book offers a deeper exploration of the characters' internal struggles and the emotional nuances of their journey.
